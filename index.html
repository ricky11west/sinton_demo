CFO CommentaryExecutive Narrative — FY 2024
Subject
Sinton Golf Course — FY 2024 Financial Summary
Prepared January 2025
Strong revenue growth of 11.4% to $742K signals healthy demand — but labor costs running above industry benchmarks and a lean cash position of $128K are the two items that require immediate attention heading into 2025.

Key Observations
  • Green fee revenue grew 13.8% — rounds are up 8.2% and average revenue per round increased $2.10, meaning the 2024 pricing adjustment is holding. This is the cleanest signal in the P&L and should be maintained in 2025.
  • Labor is the highest-risk line at 34.1% of revenue. Industry standard for comparable golf operations runs 28–32%. The $31,600 payroll increase (14.3% over 2023) outpaced revenue growth — a staffing and scheduling review needs to happen before March.
  • Pro Shop margin improved — COGS grew 10.0% against a 14.2% revenue increase, meaning the shop is buying smarter or selling through faster. Worth protecting and building on in 2025.
  • Marketing spend jumped 31.0% with no documented attribution to new rounds or memberships. Before increasing this in 2025, it should tie to a measurable channel — online tee times, social ads, or local outreach.
  • Professional services increased 33.3% with no documented scope change. This line should be reconciled — if it includes accounting or legal, those should be separated and evaluated annually.
⚠ Watch Items for 2025
  • ⚑Cash reserve at $128K is below a healthy floor. Recommend building to $150–180K by Q2 2025 to cover the seasonal dip and any unexpected equipment failure.
  • ⚑If payroll runs another 14%+ increase in 2025, net income will compress even with continued revenue growth. Staffing audit recommended before the season starts.
  • ⚑Course maintenance equipment approaching end of useful life. Capital planning should begin Q1 2025 to avoid emergency spend during peak season.
Next 12-Month Outlook
If round volume holds and pricing is maintained, 2025 revenue is on track for $800K–$820K. The path to a 13–14% net margin runs through labor discipline and controlling course maintenance costs. The single highest-leverage action is implementing a structured monthly close process — right now, financial visibility is likely lagging 30–60 days, making it difficult to catch cost overruns before they compound. TurnkeyCFO can close that gap immediately and deliver a clear financial picture to ownership every month without adding internal headcount.
